# How to Practice

Whether you're following the beginner's curriculum or the Quick Mastery streamlined curriculum, you'll find exercise chapters right after each algorithm framework explanation. Their main purpose is to help you internalize the frameworks so you can truly apply them independently when facing new problems.

Readers often ask: "Why do I understand everything when reading the explanations, but can't solve problems on my own?" It's because you haven't internalized the knowledge.

These exercises are moderately difficult problems where you can directly apply the frameworks. Through repeated practice, you can easily internalize various algorithm frameworks. That's why exercise chapters are such a crucial part of the learning process.

## How to Use Exercise Chapters

Let me introduce a practice method that I've personally verified works.

Take [Sliding Window Algorithm Core Framework](</en/algo/essential-technique/sliding-window-framework/>) as an example. The corresponding exercise chapter is [Sliding Window Algorithm Classic Exercises](</en/algo/problem-set/sliding-window/>).

First, you need to thoroughly study [Sliding Window Algorithm Core Framework](</en/algo/essential-technique/sliding-window-framework/>).

**The example problems are mainly for learning and imitation - you can follow the article's approach to write code. But next, you need to make sure you can independently solve at least one new sliding window problem.**

Don't just say you feel like you've learned it. If you don't verify this way, can you really trust yourself?

The corresponding exercise chapter [Sliding Window Algorithm Classic Exercises](</en/algo/problem-set/sliding-window/>) contains suitable practice problems. Don't look at my solutions first - try to solve them yourself. If you really can't solve the first problem, don't get stuck on it. Try the next one, and so on.

This process of forcing yourself to think might be painful, but it's the necessary path to improvement. Only through trial and error will you truly become familiar with every detail of the framework.

The problems in the exercise chapters are carefully selected framework problems - you can't possibly fail to solve even one. As long as you can independently solve at least one problem, you've passed the test. For the other problems, you can refer to my solutions to see where your thinking got stuck and what can be optimized.

## How to Review Exercise Chapters

Review is absolutely necessary. I recommend that after finishing an exercise chapter, you come back to do the exercises again after about 3 days.

Since you've already gone through them once, try not to look at my solutions when reviewing - think through the problems yourself. If you can't solve some, then look at the answers, and come back again in a few days until you can solve them all.

Don't Memorize Problems

Algorithms aren't rote memorization material. Don't try to memorize problems - it's useless. You should understand the principles of algorithms, not memorize specific solution code.

For example, don't use `<=` when reviewing just because you remember that's what you used last time. That's useless.

Forgetting the solution code is actually a good thing! That means you need to analyze it yourself and think while you write. Why use `<=`? What happens if you use `<`? You need to be able to explain the reasoning. That's what review means. Otherwise, you're just swallowing things whole and memorizing by rote, which is useless.
