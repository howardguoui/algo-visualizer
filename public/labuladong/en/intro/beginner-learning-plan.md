# Complete Learning Plan

For readers who **have enough time and are interested in algorithms** , just learn in the order of this site’s catalog. Then you can fully master all skills about data structures, algorithms, and solving problems.

## ¶More Than Just Doing Problems

I think algorithms are very good, especially for beginners, because you can get positive feedback easily: once you learn one algorithm framework, you can solve many problems quickly. This feeling of progress is very strong.

Especially for students who have not graduated, I strongly suggest you fully learn the basics of data structures and algorithms. Once you build this “framework thinking”, it will not fade away. After you start working, it can save you a lot of trouble. When you change jobs, you may not need to spend extra time preparing algorithms.

After you start working, it is hard to have large blocks of time to study and think. So for readers who already work, there is another solution: [Quick Mastery Track](/en/algo/intro/quick-learning-plan/).

Also, besides doing exercises, you can think more about the algorithms you learned. Can this framework thinking be used in real life? Can some real-life scenarios be turned into an algorithm problem? If you meet a real problem, can you abstract it and solve it with programming?

Readers who like my tutorials must like thinking, or you would not have the patience to read a tutorial with hundreds of thousands of words. But in the end, thinking must lead to real action.

Right now, you may feel happy when you solve a few algorithm problems. But after you fully master all the thinking methods on this site, you will find these problems are too easy. This small success will no longer give you strong positive feedback.

So I hope during this learning process, you can build the habit of thinking and doing things yourself. In the end, use these skills in a wider range of fields, keep curiosity about new things, and always keep moving forward.
